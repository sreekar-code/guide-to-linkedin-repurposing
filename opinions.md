## 5 Offbeat on-call rotations that work

- Follow-the-sun rotations only work when the handoff is treated as a genuine transfer, not just a change in who holds the pager
- A rotation that looks fair on paper can still burn people out if incident density is uneven across time zones or shifts
- Most teams inherit their on-call rotation structure without questioning it — the default is rarely the best fit
- Matching the rotation to how the team actually works beats matching it to how other teams work

## Understanding L1, L2, L3 escalation policy

- An escalation chain earns its cost only when expertise genuinely varies across levels — without that gap, it adds process without changing outcomes
- Most teams discover their escalation policy is outdated during an incident, not before one
- The value of a final escalation level isn't how often it fires — it's that it exists at all
- What an escalation policy says when nobody responds matters as much as what it says when someone does
