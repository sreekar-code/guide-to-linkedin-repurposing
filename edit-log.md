## Patterns from initial calibration (March 2026)

These patterns were extracted from 3 rounds of post generation and user review before the first guide was published.

- **No forced bullet points.** Bullet points are fine when they genuinely fit but using them just to break up text makes the post feel like a template. If the idea flows better as connected sentences, write it that way.
- **Paragraphs must be mobile-friendly.** One to two sentences per paragraph max. A three-sentence paragraph on desktop becomes a wall of text on a phone. Break aggressively.
- **P.S. must not be formulaic.** Do not use the same P.S. structure across posts. Do not mention the guide title in the P.S. Do not start every P.S. with "We wrote..." or "We broke down..." — vary the structure. The P.S. has one job: make the reader click.
- **Hooks should be short when possible.** Shorter hooks catch attention faster. Not a hard rule, but default to short and only go longer if the punch requires it.
- **Do not assume jargon familiarity.** Words like "handoffs", "MTTA", "handoff windows" are not universally understood. Use plain language or give one sentence of context. When in doubt, use simpler words.
- **Prefer specificity over vagueness.** "There's a rotation that helps" is weak. "One team runs their on-call on the Fibonacci sequence" is specific enough to stick. Concrete details create stronger curiosity than abstract hints.
- **The closing line must open a door, not close one.** Do not end on a neat summary. End on tension — an unresolved question, a gap, a hint that the guide contains something the reader now wants.
